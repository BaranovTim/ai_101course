Absolutely! It's a tricky concept at first. Let's break it down simply.

Imagine you are blindfolded at the top of a mountain, and your goal is to reach the lowest point in the valley. You feel the slope of the ground with your feet and take a step in the downward direction.

The Learning Rate

The learning rate is the size of the step you take.

• If your steps are too big (high learning rate), you might step entirely over the valley and end up on the other side of the mountain.
• If your steps are too small (low learning rate), it will take you a very, very long time to reach the bottom.
